What's a stress-strain curve?

January 1, 2014 by Mark

In this blog post we will explore what a stress-strain curve is and where you will encounter it in engineering.

A stress-strain curve can be curve fit using Ramberg-Osgood method. This can be extremely useful.

Osgood was a good man. His method is very helpful.

Why, you may ask? Well his equation can save a lot of time.

How is a stress-strain curve obtained?

It all starts with a simple tensile test.

How long does it take?

It doesn't take a long time at all. In fact it's pretty quick.

Insert equation here

Once you have the test data, it's a pretty painless process.

Fitting the parameters

There is a good reason that we use the 0.002 strain parameter.

  • It's standard practice
  • It's the typical definition of yield.
  • There are other reasons.

The process can be summarized as follows:

  1. Get the best data you can get your hands on
  2. Use the Ramberg-Osgood equation to come up with a curve fit.
  3. Use the curve fit in your script or fea.
